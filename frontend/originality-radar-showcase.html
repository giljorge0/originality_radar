The Idea

The inverse Akinator.

Akinator narrows toward what everyone knows. This does the opposite — it measures how far your idea sits from the dense clusters of human thinking, in a real high-dimensional vector space.

🗺️

Topology of curiosity

When humans ask similar things, that region of thought-space becomes dense. Your idea gets embedded as a coordinate and measured against every other coordinate in the database.

📐

Real cosine distance

Not vibes. Not an LLM guessing. Voyage AI produces deterministic semantic vectors. The same idea submitted twice gives nearly identical coordinates. The math is reproducible.

🌌

The map grows denser

Every submission enriches the global database. A VOID idea today (score 95) may become FRONTIER (score 78) in six months as others converge on that space. Originality is relative and evolving.

Density Scale

Where does your idea live?

Six zones. Each corresponds to a real range of cosine distances from the existing database.

SATURATED 0 – 15 Millions of people have thought this exact combination. Every startup pitch deck has it. "social media app"
DENSE 16 – 35 Well-explored territory with heavy prior art. Common combinations, well-funded companies nearby. "AI-powered CRM"
POPULATED 36 – 55 Known territory with a distinguishing angle. Prior art exists but isn't exhaustive. "dating app for readers"
SPARSE 56 – 75 Unusual domain intersection. Few have connected these dots, and none at scale. "EEG-controlled ferrofluid instrument"
FRONTIER 76 – 90 Genuinely novel. Hard to find prior art. You'd need to explain the concept from scratch to most people. "topology-aware dream compiler"
VOID 91 – 100 Alien territory. No meaningful cluster nearby. Possibly unthought of, possibly unthinkable. "???"

The Math

Scoring formula.

One equation. No learned weights, no prompt-tuned scores.

Originality Score
O(x) = (1 − (1/k) Σ cos_sim(x, xᵢ)) × 100

Where x is your idea's vector, xᵢ are the k nearest neighbors in the database, and cos_sim measures directional alignment in high-dimensional space. A score near 0 means you're pointing in exactly the same direction as the crowd. A score near 100 means you're pointing somewhere no one has pointed before.

Why Deterministic Embeddings Matter

The same idea submitted twice should produce almost identical scores. Voyage AI embeddings are stable — not sampled, not temperature-dependent. This means the map is reproducible. If your idea scores 73 today, it scores ~73 tomorrow, unless the database grows and new neighbors appear near you. The score changes only as the map fills in around you.

Stack

What's running under it.

Open source. Self-hostable. Every component is swappable.

Embeddings
Voyage AI
voyage-3-large. Deterministic, high-dimensional semantic vectors. Swappable for OpenAI or local models.
Vector DB
Supabase + pgvector
PostgreSQL with the pgvector extension. k-NN queries in SQL. Free tier covers thousands of ideas.
Narrative
Claude Sonnet
Analyzes what makes the idea common vs. novel. Generates domain-specific drift suggestions.
Backend
Node.js + Express
REST API. POST /ideas/scan, GET /ideas/map. Runs on Railway or any Node host.
Frontend
React + Vite
Canvas-based global map. Real-time score meter. Domain filter chips. Deploys to Vercel.
Projection
PCA
Principal Component Analysis reduces high-dimensional vectors to 2D for the global map canvas. Distance is preserved.
