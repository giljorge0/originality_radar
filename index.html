The math

One formula. No learned weights.

The score is pure geometry in embedding space — not a trained classifier, not a prompt asking Claude to guess.

Originality Score
O(x) = (1 − (1/k) Σ cos_sim(x, xᵢ)) × 100

Where x is your idea's vector, xᵢ are the k nearest neighbors in pgvector, and cos_sim measures directional alignment in the embedding space. Score near 0 → you're pointing in exactly the same direction as the crowd. Score near 100 → you're pointing somewhere no one has pointed before.

Why same idea → same score

Voyage AI embeddings are deterministic — not sampled, not temperature-dependent. The same text in gives the same vector out. Your score only changes as the database grows and new neighbors appear near you. This makes the map a stable reference: ideas don't drift, the terrain fills in.
